Our Acer Collection

At Acer Corner, we've spent over 25 years carefully cultivating one of the most diverse collections of Japanese maples (Acer palmatum and related species) in the region. Below is a selection of notable specimens you'll find in our garden.

Acer palmatum 'Bloodgood'

One of the most popular Japanese maples, featuring deep purple-red foliage that turns brilliant scarlet in autumn. Our specimen is over 15 years old and has developed a beautiful architectural form.

Acer palmatum 'Orange Dream'

Emerging leaves are bright orange-yellow in spring, maturing to yellow-green in summer before returning to golden orange tones in autumn. A truly three-season beauty.

Acer palmatum 'Dissectum Atropurpureum'

This weeping, lace-leaf variety has finely dissected purple foliage and creates a stunning focal point beside our main pond.

Acer japonicum 'Aconitifolium'

The Fernleaf Full Moon Maple has deeply divided leaves that put on one of the most spectacular autumn displays, turning to intense crimson and scarlet.

Acer shirasawanum 'Aureum'

Known as the Golden Full Moon Maple, this rare variety displays round, chartreuse-yellow leaves that develop remarkable orange and red autumn tints.

Acer palmatum 'Sango-kaku'

The Coral Bark Maple is prized for its bright coral-red bark that provides winter interest after its delicate green leaves (which turn golden in autumn) have fallen.

Acer palmatum 'Katsura'

Small, delicate leaves emerge orange-yellow in spring, mature to light green in summer, and return to bright orange in autumn. A compact variety perfect for smaller spaces.

Acer palmatum 'Shishigashira'

The Lion's Mane Maple has unusual curled and crinkled foliage, densely packed on its branches. Its compact growth makes it ideal for our Japanese-inspired garden section.

Acer palmatum 'Ukigumo'

The Floating Cloud Maple features variegated leaves with pink, cream, and green tones that create an ethereal effect in dappled shade.

Acer griseum

The Paper Bark Maple is known for its exfoliating cinnamon-colored bark that peels in thin, papery layers. Our specimen stands near the garden entrance, welcoming visitors year-round.

This is just a small selection of the 120+ varieties in our collection. When you visit Acer Corner, you'll discover many more rare and unusual specimens, each with its own unique characteristics.

During open days, knowledgeable garden guides are available to answer your questions about growing and caring for Acers in your own garden. We occasionally have a limited selection of young specimens available for purchase.
